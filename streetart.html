Streetart

Amsterdam has always been a city of art. But for the birth of street art, we will have to travel back in time to the 20th century. Amsterdam was a massive city for its time, with the industrial revolution going at full speed. This great technological and eco nomic advance did, however, leave plenty of poor neighbourhoods throughout Amsterdam. And in those neighbourhoods, we see the first signs of street art, with texts being written on the walls and squatters leaving graffiti on houses and cars. For street art, as we know it today, we will have to flash forward to the latter half of the 20th century. Just like it was in the first half of the century we see a rise in street art in the poorer neighbourhoods of the city: areas like the Pijp, the Jordaan, Osdorp and the Bijlmer. And especially in those neighbourhoods a new era of street art was ushered in, influenced by American pop culture. Graffiti groups like Osdorp Posse grew into hip-hop icons and stores like Henxs Montana Shop gained a cult following. The art became more as we know it today, with big letters and vibrant colours, as the artists started to take more and more creative liberties.

Amsterdam is known as a city of art and artists, but to enjoy Amsterdam's art, one can also stray away from the usual museums at the Museumplein. The beauty of street art is that it can be made on the streets and with that possibility, any street in particular. Underneath the A10 motorway, on the back of street signs and on the side of buildings and factories. You can also do a free street art tour through Amsterdam. Use the Interactive map from here and discover many secret places without any effort. Amsterdam is a city to explore. So get out there and see for yourself!
